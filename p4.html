Project #4: Exquisite Corpse

Click the screen to adjust the backdrop.

Press the space bar to reset it.

Code link: https://editor.p5js.org/ilesb520/sketches/c5FfAdDDgD

Description

Through the Exquisite Corpse exercise, the class was divided into groups of three, and we each individually sketched character ideas. This p5.js sketch was created using the head of my original character and the body and feet of my partners' two sketches.

Original Sketches

Original Collaged Reference

From this collaged reference, I was able to get a sense of how the final product would look compositionally. Creating basic and references that are realistically acheivable has been the most helpful element when creating p5.js sketches.

Design Process

After my first three assignments within p5.js, I have entered a pretty comfortable work flow, which usually requires working from the background from the foreground while following along with my reference. Near the end of my process, I realized that the sketch alone was too flat with no interactivity. To resolve this, I incorporated an interesting foreground that replicates the iPhone camera interface. This was inspired by the change in aspect ration from 400px x 400px, which we had utilized in our last three assignments, to 400px x 600px. Secondarily, I incorporated a backdrop that moves up incrimentally with the mouse click function and resets with the key pressed function.

Reflection

This project has allowed me to expand upon my knowledge with 2-D primitives as well as naming and calling my own functions. Working with other people's sketches and ideas was interesting, and what I have imagined I would render actually looks much different with the incorporation of others' works. Regarding the concepts that were covered in class in relation to this assignment, I feel very comfortable using primitives and calling functions. However, there were two issues that I ran into during this project that I could not resolve on my own. When the mouse is pressed, I originally wanted the balls to animate upwards at the click of a button; however, with my code, the balls will only move incrementally each time the mouse is clicked. Overall, this assignment was fairly straight forward and has continued to assist in my knowledge and practice of p5.js.
